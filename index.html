Terms of the Agreement

Article One:

    In this agreement, unless the context requires otherwise, the following terms shall have the meanings stated next to each of them:

  • "The Account" refers to the subject of the order in this agreement. "The Account Holder" means the client. "The Authorized Person" refers to the individual or individuals designated by the account holder to act on their behalf. "The Credit Limit" is the amount the account holder is allowed to owe the company at any time. "The Credit Period" refers to the duration within which the account holder must repay the full value of their monthly withdrawals.

Article Two:

The company shall send a monthly account statement to the account holder either in paper form or via the email address provided for correspondence in the agreement. The account holder must review the statement and notify the company of any objections within one week from the date of sending the statement. Otherwise, the statement will be considered correct and valid. The account holder undertakes to pay the full amount of the account statement within 15 days from the date the monthly statement is sent, without any delay.

Article Three:

The company has the right, at any time and without providing reasons, to suspend the execution of this agreement. The account holder must pay all outstanding amounts within 7 days from the date of being notified via the designated email for correspondence.

Article Four:

The authorized persons or any employees of the account holder responsible for purchasing and receiving goods, whether under this agreement or any subsequent document, whether on a continuous or one-time basis, and whether their authorization is through a purchase order or independently, their signature on the invoice upon receipt shall be considered as final approval from the account holder regarding the accuracy of the received quantities, their value, weight, size, and type.

Article Five:

The company may rely on instructions from authorized signatories despite any conflicting information from public announcements or the account holder's commercial registry, until it receives a written notice from the account holder revoking those instructions.

Article Six:
